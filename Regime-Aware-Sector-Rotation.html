Optimal Routing in AMM-based Ecosystems

Mathematical modelling of optimal routing in networks of Constant Function Market Makers.

Crypto Quantitative Finance

Overview

The paper studies optimal routing through networks of CFMM pools, with a focus on Uniswap V2. It formulates trade execution as an optimization problem and shows that relaxing trading constraints yields a convex program that is exact under realistic growth and saturation assumptions.

Main results

Convex duality produces an equilibrium price system, and the absence of arbitrage is shown to be equivalent to the existence of a global price certificate compatible with each pool's local marginal prices. Arbitrage is interpreted as profitable swap cycles whose returns are bounded by round-trip fees.

Numerical illustration

A triangular network of three Uniswap V2 pools (WETH/USDC, WETH/DAI, USDC/DAI) is used to compare direct, indirect and optimal split routing. For a 10 WETH order, the optimal split sends 98.22% directly and only 0.18% indirectly, yielding about 5.7 DAI more than the best single route.

Technical approach

The model relies on CFMM trading functions, transaction baskets, and convex relaxation. It is validated numerically with a general convex program solved by a conic interior-point solver, confirming that route allocation matters more than selecting a single path.
